FAQs
Q1. How can I create my own font?
You can find resources and tools on the internet to create your own font.
Q2. Why aren't mathematical equations changing to my font after uploading?
Your font may not contain glyphs for mathematical symbols. In such cases, the equations will default to a system or fallback font that includes mathematical symbols. Ensure your font includes mathematical glyphs for them to display correctly.
Q3. How do I report a problem, issue, or provide feedback?
You can contact me directly via my Email.
Q4. Can I use it on mobile devices?
Yes, you can use it on mobile devices, but for the best experience, desktop use is recommended.
Q5. How do I write mathematical equations in the input?
You can write equations in LaTeX format. They will automatically render as original equations. If they are not rendered, enclose LaTeX equations with "$" signs, for example, "$ your LaTeX equation $".
Q6. When I add an image to my paper, Why it shows as 'img1', 'img2', etc. How can I change the position of images on my paper?
When you add an image, it is referenced by its number in the input, such as 'img 1'. You can move an image to any position on your paper by typing its reference at that position in the input. To remove an image, simply delete its reference from the input.
Q7. How can I align the text with the background paper lines?
If your text does not contain any complex equations, you can align it by setting the paper line spacing and input line spacing values to be equal or in a ratio, for example, 21. Note that the values must be equal or in ratio. If your text contains equations, it cannot be aligned because equal spacing is not available for that. In that case, you have to use paper without lines.
